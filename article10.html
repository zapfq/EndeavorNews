Hope (Sowon) is a Korean film released in 2013. The movie is based on the kidnapping of Cho Doo-soon in Korea in 2008. This is a story about victims of child sexual violence and their families desperate to heal their wounds.

This is a very real, moving, tear-jerker film, it is based on a real case, it left a deep impression on the audience. It is not only the suffering of the protagonist and the darkness of society that pains the audience; there are many people like Sohara who are suffering in reality.

Even secondary school students were involved and moved the community to reduce plastic usage in the Plastic Tide Turners Challenge.

It is difficult to describe and express the overwhelming feeling of watching this film. Good men are always on the wrong side of laws that are supposed to be just and fair. Suhara is a beautiful and kind girl, people say that a good deed will be rewarded, but Suhara's kindness hurt her life. Just because she was alone on her way to school and couldn't bear to see a strange uncle in the heavy rain.

Infuriating, the reporter in the film forced his way into a hospital room and tried to interview a badly traumatised girl in order to get a first-hand story. That group of journalists completely lacked the professionalism expected of qualified journalists.

Since the film aired, many people have been talking about Sowon's case. No one can imagine what was going through the mind of a little girl like that when she was subjected to the worst malice in the world. While the perpetrator is pleading his innocence, the victim is reflecting on what went wrong. Is it wrong to want to help the uncle, or is it because she's wearing a dress today?

Sowon asked, "Did I do something wrong? Uncle asked me to give him an umbrella, I would have just walked away, but I thought I should give uncle an umbrella, so I did. But people say it's my fault. No one praises me."

The child is taught to help others when they need help, but no one tells her that people with malicious intentions will hurt her. That naive and hurt tone, in an instant, made all the women empathise. It is no longer a matter of days that women are abused.

This happened not only to Sowon, but also to many ordinary days and nights. Hence, many women feel helpless after watching this movie, because the reality has reminded us with many bloody cases: hell is empty, the devil is on earth.

"Sowon case", "bus rape in India", "Didi driver rape in China"... Too many, and women can only cry out of fear. The victim did something wrong, but malice is always unprovoked, and so is being hurt by others. But fortunately, film directors are trying to convey human warmth. Using the name "Hope" as his film title, the film also shows how Sowon's family perseveres through life's darkest moments.

Never let a child be afraid to wear a floral dress. Laws should be enforced strongly on those who prey on the weak. Yes indeed, even within civilised society, we are still following the rules of mother nature. In places where laws fail, fear should take its place. Making those perpetrators afraid to commit their deeds by punishing them with a heavy price on the courtroom, don’t even give them a chance to breathe thinking they can walk out of there in one piece.
