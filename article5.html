Has Malaysian culture really been influenced by KPOP? Let's find out.

Entertainment News by Jing Chun.

Ask young people if they like KPOP and you will find a jubilant "Yes!" from people of all genders and races.

Good-looking handsome men and women on the stage singing beautiful music, dancing a beautiful dance... Ten years ago, the Korean trend was firmly introduced to the Malaysian public by idol groups such as EXO, Girl Generation, and PSY's Gangnam Style. Even today, with the changing times and fierce competition among Korean entertainment idol groups, the groups you support may not be the first few artists, but there is no denying that Korean music has already infiltrated the lives of young Malaysians.

Ask young people if they like KPOP and you will find a jubilant "Yes!" from people of all genders and races.

YiAnn, a college student, said: "Why do I like kpop? I was attracted to the idol stage presence because compared to other genres of music that artists themselves would just stand on the stage and perform but for K-pop because they also have their own choreography as well and the items themselves dance very powerfully during the stage.”

The reason why most people like KPOP is because of the charm of the idols and the cultural influence from childhood. But some say it gives them confidence.

Zhengdong, 20, said, "Although some parents may think that KPOP has a negative influence on their children, such as blindly imitating Idol or wanting to debut in South Korea... But I think KPOP gives my generation confidence. Dancing requires a fashionable aesthetic, so I'm confident."

Jessie, a 22-year-old dancer said: "I used to have low self-esteem when I was dancing. But KPOP gave me confidence. For example, Hwasha of MAMAMOO (a female kpop group member), she gave us confidence that she can dance no matter what figure she is, and she doesn't need to care about others' opinions.”

Brian, another KPOP culture fan, said, "The biggest impact KPOP has had on me is that it has helped me lose weight. I used to weigh 100kg, but then I lost it by dancing to a KPOP song. Now I'm 70kg.”

The influence of Kpop culture on Malaysia is huge, it has influenced our food, trend, music, dance style, and so on. More and more Korean food can be found in Malaysia, and kimchi, which is often seen in TV dramas, has been imported from overseas as jars of frozen processed videos. The fashion of young people is also influenced by South Korean idol stars, which have become the latest trend. Not only that but the styles and types of music that Malaysians listen to have also increased.

Is this good or bad for us? In fact, in the era of the booming Internet, countries have long lost their mystique. Malaysia has embraced not only the Korean Wave, but also the ideas and cultures of China, Japan, Thailand, Europe, and the United States, and become more tolerant and respectful. It will be a more pluralistic era for the younger generation, and it all started because of the rise of the KPOP culture in Malaysia.

Jessie said: “I think it must influence our culture. Kpop culture must be created because it is seen by the public. We have embraced their KPOP culture, their clothes, their ideas. Some people even study hard because of their idols. These are good influences
