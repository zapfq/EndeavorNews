The situation of Malaysian people reading books.

The average Malaysian reading books is 15 books a year, every month may read one more book. According to the interim report in 2014, it can be seen that Malaysians' reading habit is not ideal, they only read two books a year seven years earlier.

Nowaday, the development of the Internet has led people to spend more time on mobile phones. When people are free, such as waiting for the bus, waiting for the subway, or taking the bus and the subway, it is obvious that most of the people are playing with their mobile phones, and it is rare to see people holding a book.

But that doesn't mean that holding a mobile phone means not reading. Nowadays, technology and the Internet are so advanced, people don't have to have paper books and can also read books, they just need to open their mobile phones, open Google and search for the book they want, and they can see it immediately.

According to studies, it has been shown that intellectually-stimulated people who read can achieve civilisation and nation-building, dispute and design ideas, which Malaysians are not doing enough.

From the news story on 4 June 2022 reported by New Straits Time (NST), the government of Datuk Seri Ismail Sabri Yaakob encouraged people to read more, and strengthened the reading culture by holding book fairs in Malaysia.

The Prime Minister said a successful nation was one that read, and in the force to improve the nation, the culture of reading should be nurtured continuously.

"The development of a country also depends on its people who are knowledgeable, positive-minded, hardworking, and competitive. Knowledge can be acquired through the practice of reading," he said.
