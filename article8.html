Reasons behind Sepang F1 hiatus and plans to bring it back.

Sports News by Fu Qi.

KUALA LUMPUR: It's been a long time since we have seen a Formula One Grand Prix hosted in one of the most prestigious circuits in Asia, The Sepang F1 Circuit, located in Sepang, Malaysia.

It's been five years since the last red lights went off in Sepang. The race ended on a high note where a young prodigy, Max Verstappen, won his 2nd ever race in Malaysia during the 2017 Grand Prix. (Redbull, 2017)

The reason was due to the gradual degradation in viewership throughout the years, starting from 1999 till 2017, but the worst came during the 2016 season, where only a total of 2.43 million viewers tuned in, which is the lowest for the Malaysia Grand Prix ever. (MSB, 2016)

As Tourism and Culture Minister Datuk Seri Nazri Abdul Aziz stated, F1 has become such a burden to host and no longer pulling in enough revenue to make it sustainable. (The Star, 2016)

"I don't think there is anywhere in the world the number of spectators for F1 has increased. So, I think that for long-term interest, it is the right thing to do," Nazri said.

On that note, another series of races known as the "MotoGP", a superbike racing event, has seen an increase in interest, and the plans are to continue to strive towards it for the Sepang Circuit.

So why the sudden interest in bringing back the prestigious event to the heart of Malaysia, Kuala Lumpur? The new political swift in powers might have a say in it.

As Tun Dr Mahathir Mohamad stated, the fresh faces in government are bringing the glory of Formula One back to Malaysia due to the boost of interest it has generated within the automotive industry. (MalayMail, 2019)

Dr Mahatir seems to have high hopes the Sepang Grand Prix being held once again will attract a large number of spectators.

“We will be able to get spectators, more than 100,000 people and that will be worthwhile for us. Besides, when we have the Grand Prix, the television stations will broadcast this all over the world, bringing about 200 million viewers,” he said.

Formula One statistics show that during the hiatus years of the Sepang Grand Prix, F1 has shown promising growth in their interest. (F1, 2020)

The global viewership shoots up to 1.922 billion, the highest peak in almost a decade and an increase of nine per cent to 2018.
